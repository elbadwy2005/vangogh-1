Vincent Van Gogh

Vincent van Gogh was a Dutch Post-Impressionist painter born in 1853, whose bold colors and expressive brushwork revolutionized modern art. Although he only painted for about a decade, he produced over 2,000 works, including around 900 paintings. Some of his most famous pieces include Starry Night, Sunflowers, and The Bedroom. Van Gogh struggled with mental illness and poverty throughout his life, and his unique style was under appreciated during his lifetime. Today, he is regarded as one of the most influential artists in history.
